Presidential Inaugurations Define January 20th as Day of New Beginnings

A date marked by historic transfers of power and landmark speeches

January 20th has become synonymous with American presidential inaugurations since 1937, when the 20th Amendment moved the ceremony from March 4th. Franklin D. Roosevelt's second inauguration that year set a tradition that continues to this day.

Among the most memorable moments: John F. Kennedy's 1961 address urging Americans to "ask not what your country can do for you," and Barack Obama's historic 2009 inauguration as the nation's first African American president. In 2021, Kamala Harris made history as the first female Vice President.

Historic Headlines
1265

First English Parliament Convenes at Westminster

Simon de Montfort summons what historians consider the first representative English parliament, laying groundwork for modern democracy.

1778

Captain Cook Makes First European Contact with Hawaii

British explorer James Cook lands on the Hawaiian Islands, naming them the "Sandwich Islands" after his patron, the Earl of Sandwich.

1892

First Official Basketball Game Played!

In Springfield, Massachusetts, Dr. James Naismith's newly invented game is played publicly for the first time. The sport will become a global phenomenon.

World Events
1841

Hong Kong Island Claimed by British Forces

The island is occupied by British troops, beginning its transformation into one of the world's greatest trading ports and financial centers.

1981

Iran Hostage Crisis Ends After 444 Days

Fifty-two American hostages are released from Iran just minutes after Ronald Reagan takes the oath of office, ending a 14-month ordeal.

1986

First Martin Luther King Jr. Day Observed

The nation celebrates its first federal holiday honoring the civil rights leader, recognizing his legacy of peace and equality.

Science & Innovation
1930

Buzz Aldrin Born - Future Moonwalker

Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin enters the world in Glen Ridge, New Jersey. He will become the second human to walk on the moon in 1969.

1996

Yasser Arafat Elected Palestinian Leader

In the first Palestinian general election, Yasser Arafat wins the presidency with 88% of the vote, marking a historic moment in Middle East politics.

2009

Obama Takes Historic Oath of Office

Barack Obama is inaugurated as the 44th President of the United States, becoming the first African American to hold the nation's highest office.
